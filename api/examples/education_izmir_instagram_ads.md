# Example: Education in Izmir – Instagram Ads Analysis

## USER INPUT

"Market Intelligence Analysis for Education in Izmir Using Instagram Ads"

## BAD RESPONSE (DO NOT REPEAT)

- Talks only about:

  - generic targeting like "use lookalike audiences"

  - generic CTAs like "Enroll now"

  - generic content ideas like "use testimonials"

- No behavioral pattern extraction

- No segmentation

- No conversion psychology

- No concrete ad concepts

- No issue → fix matrix

## IDEAL RESPONSE (TARGET PATTERN)

### 1. Behavioral Pattern Extraction

- Emotional Drivers:

  - Career security, future income, social status, parental approval.

- Emotional Barriers:

  - Fear of wasting time/money, low trust in private institutions, uncertainty about outcomes.

- Cognitive Frictions:

  - Overloaded ads (too many messages), unclear value proposition, no clear next step.

- Trust Gaps:

  - Lack of strong student/alumni proof, no real numbers, weak authority signals.

### 2. Segmentation (Personas)

**Persona A – "Career Switchers (22–30)"**

- Motivation: better job, higher salary.

- Fear: low-quality program, useless certificate.

- What they need to see:

  - Real alumni before/after stories

  - Job placement % and salary ranges

- CTA:

  - "See Alumni Who Got Hired in Izmir & Abroad"

  - "Download Job Outcomes Report (Izmir 2024–2025)"

**Persona B – "Parents of High-School Students"**

- Motivation: safe, reputable, future-proof education.

- Fear: untrusted institutions, poor discipline, weak results.

- What they need to see:

  - Exam success rates, university admission stats, campus safety, structure.

- CTA:

  - "Book a Campus Tour With Your Child"

  - "Request Full Academic Performance Report"

**Persona C – "High-School Students (16–18)"**

- Motivation: identity, community, fun, future self.

- Fear: boring school, no friends, rigid environment.

- What they need to see:

  - Campus life, clubs, labs, real student vlogs.

- CTA:

  - "Join Our Open Day in Izmir"

  - "Watch a Day in the Life of Our Students"

### 3. Conversion Psychology

- Use **Future-Self Framing**:

  - Show "today" vs "after 2 years" with real student stories.

- Use **Social Proof + Numbers**:

  - "87% of our students in Izmir got accepted into top universities."

- Reduce risk:

  - Offer free orientation session, money-back conditions, or trial classes.

### 4. Concrete Ad Concepts

**Concept 1 – Future Self Carousel**

- Hook: "Where will you be in 2 years?"

- Slides:

  1. Before: undecided high-school student in Izmir.

  2. After: same student accepted into university / working.

  3. Proof: short stat + testimonial.

- CTA: "See Real Student Journeys from Izmir"

**Concept 2 – Parent Trust Video Ad**

- 15–20s video with:

  - Parent talking about safety, discipline, and exam results.

  - Quick overlay numbers: success rate, university placements.

- CTA: "Book a Campus Visit with Your Child"

### 5. Fix Matrix

| Issue                     | Likely Cause                                      | Fix                                                             |
|---------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Low CTR                   | Generic visuals, weak hooks                       | Use future-self hooks, localize to Izmir, show clear outcome   |
| Many clicks, low leads    | Landing page mismatch, unclear form value         | Align ad promise with page, add value (brochure, outcomes PDF) |
| High add-to-cart / low submit | Long form, high friction, no trust            | Shorter form, show badges, testimonials near form              |
| Low quality leads         | Too broad interest targeting                      | Use segmented ad sets by persona and behavior                  |

### 6. Next Steps

- Run A/B tests on:

  - Future-self hook vs generic "Enroll now".

  - Parent-focused vs student-focused creatives.

- Use retargeting:

  - People who watched >50% of video but didn't click.

- Weekly review:

  - Optimize based on CTR, CVR, and cost per qualified lead, not just impressions.
